# Freelancer CRM — Capstone Project

## Overview
A web-based CRM built specifically for freelancers, developed entirely using AI tools as a capstone project to showcase product management skills and hands-on learning. The project spans three interconnected deliverables: the core CRM app, a customer support website (CSaaS) with a VAPI voice agent, and a RAG-based chatbot.

## Problem Statement
There is a gap in the CRM market — no niche CRM exists specifically tailored to freelancers. Existing CRMs are built for teams and enterprises, leaving freelancers to cobble together workflows across generic tools. The exact pain points to address will be discovered iteratively through building, learning, and studying the market with each phase.

## Target Audience
Freelancers — independent professionals who manage their own clients, projects, and pipelines. Further segmentation (industry, experience level, geography) to be refined through iteration and market study.

## Vision of Success
A polished, portfolio-ready capstone that demonstrates:
- End-to-end product management skills (ideation → strategy → execution → analytics)
- Ability to build a real product using AI-powered tools with zero coding background
- Iterative, phase-based product development discipline
- Proficiency with modern AI tooling across development, testing, design, and product thinking

The product itself should be a functional, free-to-use MLP that a freelancer could realistically adopt.

### Project Ecosystem
- **Project 1 (Core):** Freelancer CRM web application
- **Project 2 (Support Layer):** CSaaS — a customer support website for the CRM, featuring project details, features, and a help section powered by a VAPI voice agent for troubleshooting. Uses n8n workflows to perform actions.
- **Project 3 (AI Layer):** RAG-based chatbot integrated with the CRM

### Phase Plan for Project 1
- **Phase 1 (~1–1.5 months):** Build the MLP — Minimum Lovable Product. Core functionality, free for freelancers.
- **Phase 2 (~1–1.5 months):** UX improvements first, then UI polish. Both iterated together.
- **Phase 3 (~1–1.5 months):** Introduce a metrics system — product analytics, data visualization, and metric analysis.
- **Phase 4 (~1–1.5 months):** Product strategy phase — official availability, Windows and Android apps, strategic planning, and market study.

## Scope & Constraints
- **Zero budget.** Everything must be built with free tools and resources. No paid services.
- **No commercialization.** This is a learning and portfolio project, not a business.
- **No unnecessary complexity.** Avoid perfectionism — ship and iterate.
- **Solo effort.** Ganesh is the sole product owner, designer, and builder (via AI tools).
- **No coding background.** All development is AI-assisted (vibe coding).

## Context & Motivation
Ganesh is a Product Owner at a SaaS company looking to deepen his PM skills through hands-on practice. The capstone is designed to demonstrate his ability to take a product from zero to one — using AI tools to bridge the gap between product thinking and technical execution. Each phase is intentionally designed as a learning milestone, not just a feature milestone.

## Open Questions
- **What specific freelancer problems does Phase 1 solve?** The core feature set for the MLP has not been defined yet. What can a freelancer do on day one?
- **Which freelancer segment to prioritize?** Designers, developers, writers, consultants — different freelancers have different CRM needs.
- **Feature set for MLP:** What are the 3–5 core features that make this "lovable" without a defined problem?
- **Technical feasibility within zero budget:** Are all the planned tools (VAPI, n8n, Antigravity, Stitch) fully usable on free tiers for the intended scope?
- **Stitch alternatives:** Are there better free AI design tools for this use case?
- **Phase timeline validation:** Is 1–1.5 months per phase realistic given a solo, AI-assisted workflow?
- **How do Projects 2 and 3 integrate with Project 1?** What's the dependency chain and when do they start?
