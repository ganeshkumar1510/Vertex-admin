
## Tesseract: The Future of Focus for Craftsmen

---

## 1. Vision & Mission

### Vision
To liberate independent craftsmen from administrative burden, allowing them to focus on what they do best—creating exceptional work with creative freedom and peace of mind.

### Mission
Build a creative collaborator, not just a management tool. We enhance the craftsman's work by providing intelligent insights, elegant simplicity, and human-centered automation that respects their autonomy and artistry.

## 2. Who We Serve

### The Independent Craftsman Archetype

**Profile:**
- Prioritizes **quality over quantity**
- Values deep client relationships over transactional volume
- Sees their work as a craft, not just a service
- Seeks meaningful projects that align with their values
- Builds reputation through excellence, not marketing gimmicks
- Works independently or with small, select teams

**Their Pain Points:**
- Drowning in administrative tasks (invoicing, follow-ups, scheduling)
- Losing creative time to business management
- Struggling to maintain quality while managing multiple clients
- Feeling scattered across tools and spreadsheets
- Difficulty seeing the bigger picture of their business
- Anxiety about missed opportunities or forgotten tasks

**Their Aspirations:**
- Spend 80%+ of their time on craft, not admin
- Build lasting relationships with ideal clients
- Work with intention and strategy, not reactivity
- Maintain creative flow and focus
- Feel confident their business is running smoothly
- Grow sustainably without sacrificing quality

## 5. Guiding Principles

### The Three Pillars

#### 1. Simplicity Over Features
**Philosophy:** Every feature must earn its place. Complexity is the enemy of craft.

**In Practice:**
- Default to "no" for new features; say "yes" only when essential
- Remove friction, not capability
- One clear way to do each task
- Hide complexity until it's needed
- Question every field, button, and notification

**Ask:** *"Does this help the craftsman create, or does it create busywork?"*

---

#### 2. Craft Over Scale
**Philosophy:** We optimize for quality of work, not quantity of clients. Growth is a byproduct, not a goal.

**In Practice:**
- Features that support selective client relationships
- Tools for deepening existing relationships, not just acquiring new ones
- Celebrate project completion quality, not pipeline velocity
- Help users say "no" to wrong-fit clients
- Design for 10 perfect clients, not 100 mediocre ones

**Ask:** *"Does this encourage better work, or just more work?"*

---

#### 3. Insight Over Metrics
**Philosophy:** Meaningful understanding beats vanity metrics. We surface wisdom, not dashboards.

**In Practice:**
- Show patterns that inform better decisions
- Context over numbers (why, not just what)
- Qualitative insights alongside quantitative data
- Highlight what matters (client happiness, project flow) over what's easy to measure (response time, conversion rates)
- Predictive suggestions based on user's unique patterns

**Ask:** *"Does this help them understand their business, or just track it?"*

- **Clean, uncluttered interfaces** that respect cognitive space
- **Generous whitespace** that allows breathing room
- **Thoughtful typography** that's easy to read during long work sessions
- **Subtle, purposeful color** that guides without overwhelming
- **Tactile interactions** that feel handcrafted, not corporate

### Information Architecture
- **Context-aware displays** showing only what's relevant now
- **Progressive disclosure** hiding complexity until needed
- **Natural language** over jargon and industry-speak

### Interaction Patterns
- **Minimal clicks** to common actions
- **Smart defaults** based on user patterns
- **Gentle nudges**, not aggressive notifications
- **Undo-friendly** actions that allow experimentation
- **Keyboard-first** for power users who value speed

## 8. Feature Philosophy

### Feature Categories & Priorities

#### Tier 1: Craft Protection (Must-Have)
Features that **remove administrative burden** and protect creative time:
- Client communication hub (eliminate email chaos)
- Project & deadline tracking (visual, not overwhelming)
- Smart task management (context-aware reminders)
- Invoicing & payments (seamless, automatic)
- Document organization (files linked to clients/projects)

#### Tier 2: Relationship Enhancement (Should-Have)
Features that **deepen client relationships** and improve work quality:
- Client history & context (remember what matters)
- Project retrospectives (learn from each engagement)
- Relationship health indicators (who needs attention)
- Referral & recommendation tracking
- Custom workflows per client type

#### Tier 3: Strategic Insight (Nice-to-Have)
Features that **inform better business decisions**:
- Portfolio analysis (which projects bring joy/profit)
- Capacity planning (sustainable workload visualization)
- Client fit scoring (who's ideal, who's draining)
- Pricing intelligence (am I undercharging?)
- Seasonal pattern recognition

### Feature Decision Framework

Before building ANY feature, ask:

1. **Does it protect creative time?** (Tier 1 requirement)
2. **Is it simple to understand?** (5-second rule)
3. **Does it enhance craft or just track activity?**
4. **Would a craftsman miss it if removed?**
5. **Does it add insight or just data?**

If the answer to any is "no" or "maybe," the feature doesn't ship.

## 9. User Experience Principles

### 1. Invisible Until Needed
The CRM should fade into the background during creative work and emerge precisely when it's helpful.

**Examples:**
- Auto-save everything (never ask to save)
- Context-aware suggestions (not random interruptions)
- Background syncing (no loading states)

### 2. Respectful Collaboration
The system should feel like a thoughtful assistant, not a demanding boss.

**Examples:**
- Suggestions, not mandates ("You might want to..." not "You must...")
- Gentle reminders with context ("Coffee chat with Sarah was 3 weeks ago")
- Celebratory moments ("You completed this project beautifully")

### 3. Human-First Automation
Automate the tedious, amplify the personal.

**Examples:**
- Auto-generate invoice, but let user add personal note
- Suggest follow-up timing, but let user craft message
- Track time automatically, but allow manual adjustment

### 4. Progressive Learning
The system gets smarter about each user's unique workflow.

**Examples:**
- Learn preferred communication patterns
- Adapt to individual project rhythms
- Recognize client relationship patterns
- Suggest based on past successful approaches

## 11. Success Metrics (What We Actually Measure)

### User Success Indicators

**Primary Metric:**  
**Time Saved on Admin Tasks** (weekly average)
- Target: 10+ hours per week returned to craft

**Secondary Metrics:**
- **Creative Flow Protection:** Days with 3+ uninterrupted hours
- **Relationship Quality:** Client retention rate (ideal clients)
- **Decision Confidence:** User-reported clarity on business direction
- **Peace of Mind Index:** Stress reduction around business management

**NOT Measuring:**
- Total client count (we optimize for quality, not quantity)
- Revenue growth (outcome, not our focus)
- Feature usage rates (simplicity means less clicking)

### Product Health Metrics
- **Time to value:** How quickly new users experience relief
- **Daily active usage:** Are we truly helpful or occasionally useful?
- **Feature abandonment:** Which features add complexity without value?
- **Net Promoter Score:** Would craftsmen recommend to peers?

## 12. Decision-Making Framework

### When Evaluating Any Product Decision

#### The Four Questions:

1. **Does this serve the independent craftsman?**
   - If it's more useful for agencies/teams, it's not for us

2. **Does this honor the Three Pillars?**
   - Simplicity × Craft Over Scale × Insight Over Metrics

3. **Does this reject the anti-patterns?**
   - No corporate bloat, hustle culture, or soulless automation

4. **Does this enable the transformation?**
   - From admin-burdened to craft-focused

**If YES to all four:** Build it.  
**If NO to any:** Don't build it, no matter how "nice to have."
